EXPLORE A UNIQUE CULTURE

The United Kingdom is a heterogeneous country with significant cultural influences. No matter where you are from in the globe, you will feel at home in the United Kingdom. There will always be something to do in the UK, no matter where you study. When you're not studying, you may go to pubs, sports arenas, markets, concerts, art galleries and more!

STUDY LENGTH IN UK

Degrees in the United Kingdom take three or four years to complete, depending on where you study. Degrees in England/Northern Ireland, Wales, and Scotland typically take three years. A postgraduate degree is completed in one year. This implies you will pay less money on tuition. Furthermore, there are several scholarships, grants, and bursaries available from institutions and universities.

WORK LIFE

In the UK, International students are normally permitted to work up to 20 hours per week during term time and full-time outside of term time. This will enable you to obtain useful job experience, meet new people, and earn money while continuing your education. From the summer of 2021, anyone who has completed a UK degree will be eligible for the Graduate Route initiative. This allows you to stay and work in the United Kingdom for up to two years after graduating. Most university courses enable and encourage students to do an internship as part of their studies; in fact, some schools may even offer this as a module. This means you may gain experience and boost your CV before even graduating!

WHAT PROGRAMMES ARE AVAILABLE IN THE UK?

Universities in the UK typically offer 3 year undergraduate degrees and 1 year master's programmes. If you are an international student who needs to enhance your English or satisfy the requirements of a conditional offer, foundation courses and English language schools can assist you. If you are unclear about what sort of degree or course to pursue in the UK, contact our advisors for a free consultation, and our university consultants will help you locate the ideal institution, course, and location. Head to our pathway programs, university placement and young learners page to learn more on what each program can offer you.

BENEFITS OF STUDYING IN THE UK

Cost of living

Cost of living in UK is, on average, 13.1% lower than in US & Australia. Rent in UK is, on average, 33.2% lower than in US & Australia.

Graduate visa

A Graduate visa gives you permission to stay in the UK for at least 2 and up to 5 years after successfully completing a course. You can also apply for a tier 2 visa to work in the UK permanently.

WHAT IS THE AVERAGE TUITION FEE FOR INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS IN THE UK?

Undergraduate tuition prices in the United Kingdom range from £11,400 to £38,000. The average annual cost is anticipated to be roughly £22,200. postgraduate tuition expenses range between £9,000 and £30,000. The average annual cost is anticipated to be roughly £17,109. Because tuition rates vary by course and institution, we recommend that you speak with one of our advisors for more information.
